Spread the love to your peers with these kind acts!

Mentor Underclassmen

Take some time out of your day to dedicate it to underclassmen whose shoes you were in once. Help tutor them or give some tips/guidance to better their education.

Say Thank You to Teachers

People don't appreciate the educators and workers at their school enough. They work hard to give everyone the gift of knowledge, so the least you could do is show your appreciation towards them.

Greet Everyone You Pass By On Campus

This one is super simple. Just pass a short hello, or maybe even a smile and that could change the perspective of others.

Befriend Someone Who is Struggling

If you see somone who are struggling in any aspect of their school life, or just with themselves, drop by and start a quick conversation and see where that could lead to. SOmetimes getting a coffee with someone is all that they need.

Leave a kind note in/on random lockers

Write adorable and kind reminders in a small piece of paper and drop them inside random lockers or just stick it on top of the lockers. This could really unexpectedly make someone's day. You never know.

Help Collect Trash On Campus

Not enough people appreaciate the way that their school or campus looks and will throw their trash everywhere, so take the time to better your school and everyone else around you.

Befriend Someone New

See a fresh face? Talk to them! Try not to be shy and just get to know that person while being kind and welcoming to him or her.

Say Thank You to the Janitor

Janitors aren't just people who work to clean up after your mess, but they also put a lot of effort into doing it. Say thank you when you see them and tell them how much you appreaciate the work that they do for you.

Post Inspiring Quotes Around School

School should be a positive environment for yourself and others around you. In order to keep that vibe, help put up some motivational quotes in order to cheer up people who needs some motivation because that's something that students lack a lot in school nowaday.
